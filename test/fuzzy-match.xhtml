Abstract.

In this paper, we compute the Poisson cohomology groups for any Poisson Del Pezzo surface.

1 Introduction

Recently, there has been increasing interest on holomorphic Poisson structures due to their close connection to mirror symmetry []. Holomorphic Poisson structures are also known to be connected to generalized complex structures [] and biHamiltonian systems []. To a holomorphic Poisson manifold (X,π)Xπ(X,\pi), Poisson cohomology groups are important invariants. If XXX is compact, then Poisson cohomology groups are known [] to be finite dimensional.

A Del Pezzo surface is a two-dimensional Fano variety, i.e. an algebraic surface with ample anti-canonical divisor class. For the dimension reason, any holomorphic bivector field on a Del Pezzo surface is automatically a holomorphic Poisson tensor. Therefore, holomorphic Poisson structures on a del Pezzo surface should provide us with many interesting examples of holomorphic Poisson structures on compact manifolds. It is natural to ask what the corresponding Poisson cohomology groups are. In this paper, we first give an explicit description of all holomorphic bivector fields on a given del Pezzo surface XXX. The result is classical and can be found scattered, such as in []. For the reader’s convenience, in Section 2, we recall all the results, which will also be important for our computations. Our approach relies on the classification of del Pezzo surfaces: they are C⁢P1×C⁢P1CsuperscriptP1CsuperscriptP1\mathbb{CP}^{{1}}\times\mathbb{CP}^{{1}}, C⁢P2CsuperscriptP2\mathbb{CP}^{{2}} or C⁢P2CsuperscriptP2\mathbb{CP}^{{2}} blow up at rrr (1≤r≤81r81\leq r\leq 8) generic points. For the computation of Poisson cohomology groups, we use the Dolbeault resolution, i.e., the double complex as in []. Since the Poisson cohomology groups are over CC\mathbb{C} and are finite dimensional, our approach essentially reduces to the computation of their dimensions.

Various special cases of holomorphic Poisson structures considered here have appeared in literature. For instance, holomorphic Poisson structures on C⁢P1×C⁢P1CsuperscriptP1CsuperscriptP1\mathbb{CP}^{{1}}\times\mathbb{CP}^{{1}} were studied by Duistermaat [], and a certain holomorphic Poisson structure on C⁢P2CsuperscriptP2\mathbb{CP}^{{2}} appeared naturally in the work of Evens-Lu [] while studying the variety of Lagrangian subalgebras. Noncommutative Del Pezzo surfaces were studied by several authors, for instance, []. It would be interesting to explore the relationship to these works, which will be discussed somewhere else.
